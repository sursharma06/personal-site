Background

I was born in New Delhi, India and at the age of 3, my family relocated to Dubai, UAE. As far as I can remember, my childhood is filled with funny memories. Growing up in a different country allowed me to be surrounded with people of different backgrounds and languages. I remember learning Arabic from 1st grade up until 8th grade.

I remember being 12 years old when my parents broke the news to me about moving to New York. I was so happy to find out that I was going to move to the city of dreams and that I'll have the opportunity and freedom to be what I was always meant to be in life. It was bittersweet having to leave Dubai where I grew up and start a new life. Moving to the US came with it's own challenges. It was a culture shock for my whole family and not knowing the language really well was hard on me.

After high school, I went on to college not having a clear picture in mind as to what I wanted to be. I felt lost in the crowd and even though I did well in my chosen major at the time, it definitely wasn't where my heart was. After working in a career field where I knew I did not belong, I've finally gotten the courage to take a leap of faith and pursue a new passion. A passion for coding.

It took me a few months of research and reading articles on where to start since it is a completely new career option for me. I started out with Codecademy courses on HTML and CSS. I loved learning computer-speak and being able to get an output right away felt rewarding. I took a prep course in JavaScript which really helped solidify my decision to pursue web development.

This is how I felt when I got accepted to Galvanize and I feel like I've chosen the correct curriculum for me. I am really excited to begin the Web Development program at Galvanize. I'm ready to take on the challenges that will come during the learning process and grow as a developer. I am also looking forward to meeting new people and working together as a team.
